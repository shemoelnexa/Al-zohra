Freehold Property in Ajman — What Foreign Buyers Need to Know

Al Zorah Sales Team
25 April 2026 7 min read

Can Foreigners Buy Property in Ajman? The Short Answer

The short answer is yes: foreign nationals can buy property in Ajman, provided they purchase within a designated freehold zone. Al Zorah is one such zone. It is a fully freehold community, open to buyers of every nationality, granting the same ownership rights that UAE residents hold — full title to both land and property, with no expiry and no requirement to be a resident. If you are researching freehold property in Ajman, this guide explains exactly how that ownership works, who is eligible to buy, which documents you will need, and what the purchase process at Al Zorah involves from enquiry to registered title.

What Freehold Ownership Means in the UAE

Before weighing your options, it helps to be precise about the terms, because UAE property law recognises several distinct forms of tenure that are easy to confuse.

Freehold

Freehold is full, permanent ownership of both the land and the property built on it, with no expiry date. As of 2026 under current Ajman real estate regulations, a freehold owner can sell, rent, gift or pass the property to their heirs without seeking developer approval. This is the tenure that applies to every home at Al Zorah, including the luxury villas UAE buyers most often ask about.

Leasehold

Leasehold is a long lease — typically 99 years — that grants use of a property without ownership of the underlying land. It exists in parts of Ajman, but it is not what Al Zorah offers.

Musataha

Musataha is a lesser-known right allowing a party to use and develop land for a defined period. It is frequently mistaken for ownership; it is not, and it is not the basis on which Al Zorah is sold. All Al Zorah transactions are registered with ARRA, the Ajman Real Estate Regulatory Agency — not DLD, which governs Dubai. Many international buyers know only DLD, so this distinction is worth noting early.

Ajman's Freehold Zones — Where Is Al Zorah?

Rather than describe Ajman's freehold map in general terms, it is worth being specific about where Al Zorah sits. Al Zorah is a designated freehold zone, established under an Ajman Emiri Decree. That designation is what allows foreign nationals to hold full title here.

Every transaction is registered with ARRA and completed under a single, government-unified sales and purchase agreement (SPA). Foreign buyers purchase on exactly the same title-deed basis as UAE nationals — the same document, the same rights, the same register. There is no separate, lesser category of ownership for non-nationals.

Foreign Buyer Eligibility — Who Can Purchase

Eligibility at Al Zorah is deliberately broad. As of 2026 under current Ajman real estate regulations:

  • Any foreign national may purchase freehold — there are no nationality restrictions.
  • Both UAE residents and non-residents can buy; you do not need to live in the UAE to own here.
  • There is no minimum residency requirement and no minimum stay attached to ownership.
  • Companies can purchase through recognised UAE corporate structures — a route where independent legal advice is strongly recommended before you commit.
  • GCC nationals and UAE nationals hold the same rights as any other buyer; at Al Zorah there is no distinction in tenure between nationalities.

If you are buying from abroad, our team can walk you through the steps that apply specifically to international buyers, and confirm what your particular situation requires.

Documents Required to Purchase as a Foreign Buyer

The paperwork for a freehold purchase in Ajman is straightforward, though the exact list depends on how you are buying and whether finance is involved. Requirements can change, so treat the following as a guide and confirm the current checklist with the sales team or your legal counsel before you proceed.

Typical documents

  1. Passport copy — for every buyer named on the title.
  2. UAE entry stamp or residence-visa copy — if you are a UAE resident.
  3. Source-of-funds declaration — an anti-money-laundering (AML) requirement that applies to all property purchases in the UAE.
  4. No Objection Certificate (NOC) from your employer — where you are purchasing as an employee; this is not always required.

If you are financing the purchase

Buyers using a mortgage should also expect to provide a bank pre-approval letter and to complete additional know-your-customer (KYC) checks, which vary from one lender to the next.

Al Zorah uses a government-unified SPA, which means the developer and ARRA handle registration directly. In practice this streamlines the process compared with the developer-specific agreements used elsewhere, giving foreign buyers a single, consistent contract and a clear path to registered title.

Freehold vs Leasehold — Why the Distinction Matters for Resale

The freehold-versus-leasehold distinction is not academic — it shapes what you can do with the property later, and it is where the practical value of freehold becomes clear.

Freehold is easier to finance: banks prefer full title as collateral, which widens your mortgage options. Resale is cleaner too — with no developer approval to seek and no lease term winding down, a freehold home changes hands like any other outright asset. For international owners, passing the property to heirs is handled through standard inheritance documentation rather than a lease assignment.

Freehold is also the specific status that unlocks Golden Visa eligibility. A qualifying freehold purchase at or above AED 2 million makes the buyer eligible for the UAE's 10-year Golden Visa — a leasehold unit does not qualify. That single fact is why, for most international buyers weighing the investment case in Ajman, freehold is not merely preferable but decisive.

Freehold is the status that turns a purchase into an asset you can finance, resell, pass on — and use to qualify for a 10-year Golden Visa.
