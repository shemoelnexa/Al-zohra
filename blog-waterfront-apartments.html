Waterfront Apartments in Ajman — Creekside and Marina Living Explained

Al Zorah Sales Team
07 June 2026 6 min read

Not every waterfront view is the same. At Al Zorah, the search for waterfront view apartments leads to two very different addresses — one drawn along a quiet tidal creek, the other framing an active marina. Both sit within the same 12 square-kilometre masterplan, share the same golf, beach and wellness amenities, and answer the same question buyers keep asking: what does it actually feel like to live with the water at your door? This guide sets the two side by side so you can choose the waterfront apartment that fits the life you want, not just the view on the brochure.

Creekside vs Marina Living — Two Different Waterfront Experiences

The phrase waterfront apartments covers a wider range of experiences than most buyers expect. At Al Zorah the difference is unusually clear-cut. Creekside Residence looks out over a calm, mangrove-fringed creek — still water, soft light, a slower rhythm. Gateway Porto Al Zorah sits at the community's marina, where berthed yachts, promenade dining and the movement of the harbour set the tone. Same emirate, same estate, two distinct answers to what a home by the water should be.

Because both developments belong to the same validated cluster of apartments by waterfront demand, the real decision is rarely about amenity or ownership — those are shared across the community. It comes down to the immediate outlook and the pace of daily life. The sections below take each development in turn, then help you self-select between them.

Creekside Residence — Layouts and Views

Creekside Residence is the quieter of the two waterfront addresses. Apartments are oriented toward the tidal creek, so the primary living spaces and balconies open onto still water rather than a busy frontage — the kind of water front apartment that reads as a retreat first and an address second. The result is a more residential, self-contained character: calm mornings over the creek, and a sense of privacy that suits owners who value stillness above spectacle.

Layouts are designed around that outlook. Floor plans prioritise the creek-facing aspect, drawing daylight and the water view deep into the main rooms, with generous glazing and outdoor terraces that make the view part of everyday living rather than an occasional glance. Configurations span from efficient one-bedroom homes through to larger family layouts; the exact mix and sizes vary by release phase and are confirmed on enquiry.

What sets Creekside apart is its neighbour: the protected mangrove reserve. Living beside a conservation area gives the development a genuine quiet-living differentiator — birdlife, sheltered water and a landscape that stays low-rise and green. For buyers who picture their waterfront apartment as a calm counterpoint to a busy working life, Creekside is the natural fit.

Gateway Porto Al Zorah — Marina Frontage Apartments

Gateway Porto Al Zorah is the more active, more social waterfront proposition. Where Creekside faces still water, Gateway Porto faces the marina — apartments are positioned to frame the harbour, the berthed yachts and the promenade below, so the view carries movement and life. These are waterfront apartments for buyers who want their home to sit at the centre of things rather than apart from them.

The layouts reflect that outward-facing orientation. Living spaces and balconies are angled toward the marina frontage, making the water and the promenade an extension of the home, with plans that suit both entertaining and easy lock-up-and-leave ownership. As with Creekside, unit sizes and the available configurations shift by phase and are confirmed directly by the sales team.

Position is Gateway Porto's story. Its proximity to the golf club and to yacht berthing makes it the natural choice for an active, social waterfront lifestyle — a short walk to the water's edge, dining along the promenade, and the option of keeping a boat close to home. Against Creekside's quieter, creek-facing calm, Gateway Porto is deliberately the livelier address.

Creekside is for the buyer who wants the water to be still. Gateway Porto is for the buyer who wants the water to be alive. The amenities behind them are the same — the view and the pace are not.

Which Waterfront Apartment Suits Your Lifestyle

The most useful way to choose between two strong waterfront view apartments is not to re-list their features but to match each to a way of living. Both are excellent homes; the right one depends entirely on how you want your days by the water to feel.

Creekside Residence — for calm, residential living

  • Families and buyers who want a quiet, low-rise setting beside the mangrove reserve.
  • Owners prioritising stillness, privacy and a slower daily rhythm over a busy frontage.
  • End-users looking for a genuine home rather than a scene — a creek-facing retreat.

Gateway Porto Al Zorah — for active, social living

  • Buyers drawn to marina energy, promenade dining and being close to the action.
  • Yacht owners and enthusiasts who value proximity to berthing and the golf club.
  • Those who want an easy, sociable base with the harbour as their everyday backdrop.

Amenities Shared Across Both Communities

One point matters more than any other when choosing between these two addresses: the amenities are community-wide. Whichever waterfront apartment you select, you draw on exactly the same Al Zorah lifestyle — so the choice is about your immediate view and position, not about what you can access.

  • Golf — the championship Nicklaus-designed golf course and clubhouse, open to residents across the estate.
  • Beach — direct access to Al Zorah's protected shoreline and beach facilities.
  • ZOYA Wellness — the ZOYA wellness resort and spa, a short distance from both developments.
  • Dining and marina — the marina promenade, cafés and restaurants that anchor community life.

Because these are shared, a Creekside owner is never further from the golf, beach or dining than a Gateway Porto owner — the practical difference between the two is what you see from your balcony and the pace of the water beyond it. That is why the decision is best made on lifestyle and outlook, with amenity access taken as a given.
